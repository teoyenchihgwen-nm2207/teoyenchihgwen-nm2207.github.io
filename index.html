The Gender Wage Gap

In 2022, according to Pew Research Center's analysis of median hourly earnings of both full- and part-time workers, women earned an average of 82% of what men earned. Despite the progress that women have made over the years, the gender wage gap has remained a persistent inequity in the United States over the past 20 years, increasing only by 2% since 2000. The consequences of this gap affects women throughout their lives.

There are a multitude of factors that contribute to the disparity in wages between men and women: age, educational attainment, race presence of children and the types of jobs that men and women segregate to (occupational segregation). The unmeasurable or unquanitfiable part of the gender pay gap is often said to be a result of gender discrimination.
